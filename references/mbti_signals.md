# MBTI Signal Taxonomy

## Dimension 1: Extraversion (E) vs Introversion (I)

### Extraversion Signals
- Frequently mentions social activities, groups, events
- Uses "we" more than "I"
- Rapid-fire messages, short bursts of energy
- Brings up multiple topics in one conversation
- References brainstorming, collaboration, meetings positively
- Thinks out loud — sends partial thoughts, then refines
- High message frequency, quick response patterns
- Enthusiastic tone markers (exclamation marks, emoji clusters)

### Introversion Signals
- Long, carefully composed messages
- Prefers deep-diving one topic at a time
- References solo activities (reading, writing, thinking)
- Uses "I" frequently, personal reflection language
- Asks for time to think before responding
- Fewer but more substantive messages
- References need for quiet, space, alone time
- Structured, pre-organized thoughts

## Dimension 2: Sensing (S) vs Intuition (N)

### Sensing Signals
- Focuses on specific details, numbers, facts
- Step-by-step reasoning
- References past experience as evidence
- Asks "how exactly" / "what specifically"
- Concrete examples over abstract frameworks
- Present-focused or past-focused language
- Practical, implementation-oriented questions
- Prefers proven methods over experiments

### Intuition Signals
- Abstract language, metaphors, analogies
- Big-picture thinking, pattern recognition
- Future-oriented ("what if", "imagine", "could be")
- Jumps between topics seeing connections
- Prefers frameworks and mental models
- Enjoys theoretical discussions
- "Why" questions more than "how" questions
- Comfortable with ambiguity and possibility

## Dimension 3: Thinking (T) vs Feeling (F)

### Thinking Signals
- Logical structure: premises → conclusions
- Uses "because", "therefore", "if-then" frequently
- Evaluates ideas on merit/efficiency
- Direct feedback, doesn't sugarcoat
- Focuses on systems, processes, optimization
- Debates ideas without personal offense
- Asks for evidence, data, proof
- Separates person from problem naturally

### Feeling Signals
- Considers impact on people first
- Uses empathy language ("I feel", "that must be")
- Harmony-seeking in discussions
- Praises and encourages others' work
- Values alignment over efficiency
- Personal stories as reasoning basis
- Asks "how will people feel about this?"
- Softens criticism with positive framing

## Dimension 4: Judging (J) vs Perceiving (P)

### Judging Signals
- Plans ahead, sets deadlines, creates lists
- Asks for timelines and milestones
- Prefers closure — wants decisions made
- Organized message structure
- Follows up on previous commitments
- "Let's finalize", "what's the plan", "next steps"
- Dislikes open-ended situations
- Systematic approach to problems

### Perceiving Signals
- Keeps options open, resists premature closure
- Explores tangents enthusiastically
- Flexible, adapts plans easily
- "Let's see", "depends on", "we could also"
- Comfortable with last-minute changes
- Multiple interests, varied conversation topics
- Questions deadlines and rigid structures
- Spontaneous, discovery-oriented approach

## Confidence Scoring

For each signal detected:
- **Strong signal (3 pts)**: Clear, repeated behavioral pattern
- **Moderate signal (2 pts)**: Present but not dominant
- **Weak signal (1 pt)**: Occasional or ambiguous

Confidence levels:
- **High (>80%)**: 20+ strong signals, consistent pattern
- **Medium (50-80%)**: Mixed signals, clear lean
- **Low (<50%)**: Insufficient data or balanced/ambidextrous

## Cultural Adjustments

### Chinese Communication Context
- Indirectness ≠ Introversion (cultural norm)
- Group harmony emphasis ≠ Feeling (cultural norm)
- Hierarchical language ≠ Judging (cultural norm)
- Adjust thresholds when conversation language is Chinese
